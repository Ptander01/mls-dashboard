This report details the findings of open-source intelligence (OSINT) research conducted on the Stargate data center project in Abilene, Texas. The research focused on three key areas: entity resolution and macro investment, development timelines and permitting, and capacity and infrastructure.

## Domain 1: Entity Resolution & Macro Investment

The primary shell entity associated with the Stargate project is **Rolling Plains EV Stations LLC**. However, this entity is part of a larger, more complex network of companies. **Lancium LLC** appears to be the initial developer and landowner, with tax documents listing Lancium, Rolling Plains EV Stations LLC, and Lancium Childress LLC collectively as the "Owner" [1].

The project has evolved to include several major technology companies. **Crusoe Energy Systems** is now leading the development and construction of the data center campus. The primary tenants and partners include **OpenAI**, **Oracle**, and **Microsoft**, with **SoftBank** also being mentioned in early reports [2].

The total aggregate acreage of the campus is reported to be over 900 acres, with an estimated total investment of $500 billion [2].

## Domain 3: Capacity & Infrastructure

The Stargate data center campus is one of the largest in the world, with a total projected capacity of **2.1 GW**. The project is being built in phases, with an initial 200 MW deployed, and a new 900 MW campus being developed for Microsoft. The original 1.2 GW interconnect was approved by the **Electric Reliability Council of Texas (ERCOT)**, the utility provider for the region [13, 14].

The infrastructure is designed for high-density AI workloads, with multiple data halls under construction. The first power date for the second phase of construction is estimated to be in **mid-2026** [5].

## Fayetteville, GA (Fairwater)

## Executive Summary

This report details the findings of open-source intelligence (OSINT) research into the data center development site in Fayetteville, Georgia, known as "Fairwater" or "Project Excalibur". The research focused on three key areas: entity resolution and macro investment, development timelines and permitting, and capacity and infrastructure. The primary entities involved are QTS, a data center developer owned by Blackstone, and Microsoft, which is operating an AI "superfactory" on the site. The campus is a massive, multi-phase development with a significant power capacity and a multi-billion dollar investment.

## Entity Resolution & Macro Investment

The data center campus in Fayetteville is a joint effort between QTS and Microsoft. QTS, a subsidiary of Blackstone, is the primary developer and owner of the land. Microsoft is a key tenant, operating its "Fairwater" AI data center on the site. The project is being developed under at least two shell entities: **QTS Fayetteville I DC1-2 LLC** and **QTS Eastwood, LLC**.

The total planned campus spans at least **928 acres**, comprised of a 615-acre parcel for the initial "Project Excalibur" and an additional 313-acre site for the "ATL2 East" expansion. The initial land acquisition for the 615-acre site in August 2022 was for **$153.8 million**, indicating a much larger total investment for the entire project.

## Capacity & Infrastructure

The Fayetteville data center campus is designed to be one of the largest in the world. The initial "Project Excalibur" phase is planned to have a capacity of **250 MW**. However, reports suggest the total capacity of the campus could exceed **1.4 GW (1400 MW)**. Microsoft's Fairwater facility is a key component of this, featuring a two-story design with advanced liquid cooling and a dedicated AI Wide Area Network (AI WAN) connecting it to other Microsoft AI data centers. The facility is equipped with NVIDIA GB200 NVL72 rack-scale systems.

The site is served by **Georgia Power** and **Coweta-Fayette EMC**. The first power date for the campus was in **October 2025**, when the Microsoft Fairwater facility came online.

The research focused on three key areas: entity resolution and macro investment, development timelines and permitting, and capacity and infrastructure. The primary entities involved are **Somerset Operating Co LLC**, the owner of the site, and **TeraWulf**, the developer and operator of the data center. The project involves the conversion of a former coal power plant site into a large-scale data center campus with a projected capacity of up to 750 MW.

## 2. Entity Resolution & Macro Investment

The Lake Mariner data center is being developed by **TeraWulf** on land owned by **Somerset Operating Co LLC**. While Somerset Operating Co LLC is the shell entity on record, TeraWulf is the parent company driving the project. This is evidenced by numerous press releases and public filings. Google has also taken a 14% stake in TeraWulf, indicating significant interest from major technology players.

The total investment in the project is substantial, with TeraWulf announcing a proposed offering of **$3.2 billion** to finance the expansion of the Lake Mariner campus. The campus itself has been expanded to **157 acres** through a long-term ground lease agreement between TeraWulf and Somerset Operating Co LLC.
