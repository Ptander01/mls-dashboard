# Neumorphic Fader Reference Notes

The reference image shows vertical mixer faders with these key visual properties:

The track/groove is a long rounded-end channel pressed INTO the surface. It has a soft inner shadow along the top-left edge (darker) and a subtle highlight along the bottom-right edge (lighter), creating the classic neumorphic "concave" or "pressed" illusion. The groove color is slightly darker than the background surface, with a matte finish. The groove has rounded capsule ends.

The knob/button sitting on the track is a raised circular element with a metallic/matte white appearance. It has a cross-hatch or four-petal texture carved into its surface, creating visual interest. The knob casts a soft shadow below it onto the track. The knob has a bright specular highlight on the upper-left quadrant and darker shading on the lower-right, giving it a convex 3D dome shape.

Below each knob, the groove shows a colored fill (green, yellow, pink, cyan, dark gray, amber) that represents the "value" — the portion of the track from the bottom up to the knob position. This colored fill sits inside the groove and respects the groove's inner shadow treatment.

For the horizontal dumbbell chart adaptation: the groove runs horizontally as the full track, the two endpoints (Home green, Away red) are the raised knobs, and the gap between them gets a subtle colored fill inside the groove.
