# Wisconsin Cluster Research Notes

## Entity Resolution
- **Primary Entity**: Microsoft Corporation (direct purchases, not through shell company for Mount Pleasant)
- **Related Entity**: VDC Wisco Realty Investments 1 LLC — shell for Vantage Data Centers (Port Washington, separate project)
- **Note**: Microsoft purchased land directly in its own name in Mount Pleasant, unlike typical hyperscaler pattern
- **Confidence**: High — Microsoft publicly announced and confirmed all purchases

## Land Acquisition

### Phase 1 — Initial Purchase
- 315 acres in Mount Pleasant, TID 5 (former Foxconn/Wisconn Valley)
- Purchase price: $50,085,000
- Purchased from: Village of Mount Pleasant
- Date: May 25-30, 2023
- Location: East section of Area III of Tax Increment District No. 5

### Phase 2 — Additional Land
- 166 acres (five parcels) at 12123 Louis Sorenson Road
- Purchased from: Peter Zenner Family Farm LLC
- Purchase price: $43,462,548
- Date: September 2024
- Additional parcels purchased in July 2024 (per WPR)

### Total Land Holdings
- Microsoft's purchase included nearly 1,000 acres total
- Expansion plans cover 1,300+ acres across two new campuses

## Development Timeline
- March 28, 2023: Microsoft announces agreement to purchase land in Mount Pleasant
- April 19, 2023: Microsoft agrees to buy $50M Foxconn parcel
- May 25-30, 2023: $50M land purchase of 315 acres completed
- September 2023: Microsoft breaks ground on $1 billion data center
- March 2024: We Energies plans $335M substation on property
- May 2024: Microsoft announces $3.3B total investment, AI co-innovation lab
- July 2024: Additional land purchases
- September 2024: Microsoft buys another 166 acres for ~$50M
- 2024-2025: 2,300 union construction workers on site
- May 22, 2025: PSC approves We Energies plan for two new gas plants (~$1.5B) to serve data center
- September 18, 2025: Microsoft announces "Fairwater" — world's most powerful AI datacenter
- January 20, 2026: Microsoft proposes $13.3B expansion (15 new data centers)
- January 27, 2026: Mount Pleasant Village Board approves site plans for 15 new data centers
- February 2026: We Energies seeks new rate structure for data centers at PSC

## Capacity & Infrastructure
- Phase 1 capacity: 450 MW
- Total planned: significantly larger with 15 additional data centers
- Utility Provider: **We Energies** (WEC Energy Group subsidiary)
- We Energies building substation on property ($335M investment)
- PSC approved two new gas plants (~1,200 MW total) to serve data center demand
- Three new electrical substations planned for expanded campus
- Water usage: 2.8 million gallons/year for Fairwater campus (8 million gallons/year per other estimates)

## Campus Details
- **Fairwater** — name of the first data center campus (Braun Road)
- **Durand Avenue Site**: Up to 9 data centers + 96,000 sq ft office, 5.2M sq ft total, 791 acres, est. value $7.97B
- **International Drive Site**: 6 data centers + 74,000 sq ft office, 3.5M sq ft total, 530 acres, est. value $5.33B
- Combined: 8.7 million sq ft of new construction
- First phase expected online 2026
- Contractors: Walsh Construction, Kiewit

## Utility & PSC Details
- PSC approved We Energies proposal for two new gas plants (~$1.5B): May 22, 2025
- Gas plants located in Oak Creek and Town of Paris
- Total new capacity: 1,200 MW
- PSC Docket: 05-CE-156 (gas plants) and 6630-TE-113 (rate structure)
- We Energies $335M distribution substation on Microsoft property (announced March 2024)
- We Energies November 2025: additional $5.5B expansion plan including 1.1 GW gas plant in Paris + solar + battery
- Substation status: Under Construction
- First power date: Early 2026 (Phase 1)
